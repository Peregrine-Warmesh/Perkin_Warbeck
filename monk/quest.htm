The Quest

Monktacus has undertaken a quest to find Monkey's family. All that Monktacus knew at the start of the quest was that monkey had an idyllic childhood on the banks of the Ganges near a temple.

There are many temples in India and many on the banks of the Ganges. Monktacus knew where monkey joined the circus, so his home had to be upstream from there. Monktacus has purchased a boat and is proceeding up river like Martin Sheen in Apocalypse Now.

The first temple he visited was the Golden Temple at Amritsar. The monks there were so taken with Monktacus that they asked him to join them. He refused, being essentially warlike and not suited to the mystical path. In fact he is not all that sure about Monkey's frequent visits to the Astral Plane.

 
 
In conversation with the Holy Men it became apparent that this was not the temple near monkey's childhood home, but one of the very old monks thought he remembered monkey's family visiting once and stealing all the bananas from an open air offering to Shiva. 
He said one of the other monks from the time might remember better but he had gone off to stand on one leg in the Himalayas.
Monktacus was on the point of despair when a blind beggar who had overheard remarked that in his family, there was a tale of a very cheeky monkey living nearby. The beggar had been born 15 miles up river, so Monktacus set off again like Martin Sheen in Apocalypse Now 
Monktacus didn't particularly fancy going all the way to the source of the Ganges, but he was prepared to if that was what it took. The first Holy man he found, had been holding his arm in the air for ten years.
This was not exactly the same as standing on one leg, but to Monktacus with his sophisticated Roman ways it seemed similar. 
"Hic Haec Hoc" he started. The Holy man interpreted this as "Where can I see the most beautiful temples in India?" and sent Monktacus 5 miles up river

 

Monktacus was stunned by the beauty of it all, but he was hardly getting any nearer finding Monkey's family. He realised that he would need an interpreter who spoke both Latin and Punjabi. This would be a tricky task, but apparently some of the Saddhus as the Holy Men were called , could go into a trance and speak all the tongues of the world.
 
The next Holy man that Monktacus found was already in a trance
 
So Monktacus experimentally addressed him in Latin ; "Quo Vadis Mater Pater Simius Improbus "?
The man leapt to his feet and gestured way into the distance, where the Himalayas rose from the Gangetic Plain.
Gazing at these mountainous behemoths, Monktacus realised that he was in for an epic journey. But he couldn't understand why a troop of hungry monkeys would remove themselves from a certain source of food namely the constant stream of pilgrims that attends a Ganges temple, and take themselves to the arid high terrain of the Himalaya. 

Monktacus squinted again at the panorama on the horizon. The shapes of the peaks rung a distant bell. Grabbing his copy of "a soldier's almanac of the known world, he thumbed quickly to the chapter entitled "skylines and landscapes". Thsi chapter was designed so that a soldier, transported to anywhere in the world could quickly orientate himself by referring to its pages. Monktacus had made good use of it recently when in Afghanistan, identifying the silhouettes of the Hindu Kush. "Nanda Devi" he muttered. Monktacus had been endlessly badgered by Monkey on the subject of the beauty of the Indian Himalayas and the Nanda Devi Sanctuary in particular. Oft-times when on manoeuvres, Monkey had entertained Montacus doing "mountain charades". Monktacus, likewise could do the Alps due to his crossing with Hannibal, and the seven hills of Rome.
Monktacus had not survived the intrigue and internecine skullduggery of the Roman Senate without developing a brain like a rat trap. Nanda Devi is part of the same group as Hanuman, the peak of the Monkey God and Changabang, a peak whose name in fact resembles more than coincidence ought to allow, the name of THE Monkey Go~~d. There was nothing Monktacus could put his finger on, but there seemed too much co-incidence. "We must set off for the Garhwal Himalaya", he told the holy man chucking him a roman coin.

15 days later


Monktacus breathed heavily. In the thin air of the high Himalaya, no effort came easily. He had crossed the Alps with Hannibal, but no one reared in Rome has the physiology for the rarified air at 20,000 feet. He was aware, from occasional satellite phone calls from Trumpet, that Monkey was distraught back in Monkeytown. The twin barbs of separation from Monktacus and separation from his natural family were almost too much to bear. But Monktacus task had become awesome. It appeared from various conversations along the banks of the ganges, that Monkey's original family had set off for the Nanda Devi sanctuary, believing that there were people there for whom monkeys were the highest form of life and that among these people they could live an idyllic existence.
Monktacus had wasted no time in hiring a team of Sherpas and some yaks. He was used to ordering men, but the Sherpas were a proud race and drove a hard bargain. The Sirdar or head Sherpa was a wiry and fantastically hardy man called Ang Temba.
 
Porter and yak
Ang Temba

Monktacus would not have set off on such a quest without the highest degree of confidence. Within 5 miles of his journey, they had passed some wall writings which confirmed in the strongest possible terms, the truth of his information.
 
"These writings were dictated by a blind beggar. They deal with the exodus of a large family of very cheeky monkeys, formerly of the Ganges, now in transit to the Nanda Devi Sanctuary, to live in peace with humans, and to promote the importance of monkeys in the created order"
