Monkey town: a treatment for a TV series in 8 parts.

Cast:
Monkey:
The lead character. Has led a charmed life. Raised in India, on the banks of the Ganges where he had an idyllic childhood with this many siblings. Tormented the monks of the nearby tample with his tricks and mischief. One day while playing in the Ganges, was grabbed by a crocodile and washed many miles downstream. Might have died but for the intervention of Trumpet who was basking in the shallows and saw off the crocodile. 

Sadly Monkey had no idea how to get back home and so has become an orphan. Latterly his lack of contact with his family has caused him such distress that Monktacus has gone of f on a quest to find them. Monkey and Trumpet joined circus together. Learned many tricks in circus, but unfortunately on bankruptcy, kindly circus owner had to sell monkey to zoo. Using agility and tricks learned in circus monkey escapes from zoo and makes way to monkeytown (a thinly disguised Glasgow). Main job is as DJ in "The Jungle" a club. Monkey sits behind a DJ counter from which he dispenses such music as "yes we have no bananas" "apeman" "the lion sleeps tonight".
The singular take on glasgow which is monkeytown's central motif is that the trendy and cool clubbers shun 180 bpm music clubs such as the Volcano to come and listen to monkey. Some of them call him a genius. Behaviour borders on arrogance but saving grace is hint that Monkey knows he is at it.

Most of the time Monkey is happy-go-lucky. Cheeky and irreverent to all bar Monktacus and Trumpet. Although he is obviously very fond of Monkey girlfriend he taunts her mercilessly about her imbecility, her "foetid smell" and regularly demands "who are you?" even when it is not obvious that he has just been to the astral plane. Similarly , although he is clearly fond of Monkey Slave, it is not uncommon for Monkey to tease or boss him about.

Occasionally Monkey appears brooding and preoccupied. The absence of Monktacus can cause this, especially if military matters are being seen to without Monkey. "Monktacus" he will plaintively cry. Lately he has been brooding over his lost family (see the Quest)

Girl Monkey
Elegant, attractive female monkey. Graduated Glasgow University in 1982 etc…Frequently seen shopping. Dodgy table manners. Will be required to perform sex scenes with Monkey. Frequently seen munching on a potato.
Characteristic behaviour: moving and grooving in the monkey girlfriend style, fighting with inanimate objects such as the ceiling.
Jana Nachanoo
Elegant attractive female monkey. Not on screen much. Encountered mainly through prolonged phone calls from GM.(often on the mobie) However despite this , she is in fact the progenitor of the whole monkey concept and this should be conveyed by occasional flashbacks to her screeching "Nachanoo". Ambiguous relationship with GM. Sometimes appear like sisters sometimes like mother/daughter. Very naughty monkey. Appears to live with someone called "Molly" to whom she is very michievous. 
Jana has a very important job, although not as important as Trumpet. Lately this has taken her regularly to Brazil, where she has been mistaken for "the Munk from Ipanema". her job there is to re-brand the banana which caused trouble with Monkey who thinks the banana, being perfect, needs no such footering.

Trumpet

An elephant. "the noblest pachyderm of them all" 
Monkey claims to have delivered Trumpet but  this is slightly at odds with the story that they had never met until Monkey and the crocodile washed past trumpet in the Ganges. 
They were together in the jungle and circus, but lost each other for a long time. Now reunited. 

 
Trumpet successfully contested the parliamentary seat for Govan in the 2000 election. Monkey was his agent with the rallying cry "vote for Trumpet". 

Tony Blair was quick to realise the qualities of this new MP and Trumpet has been much in demand in the international crisis. He has spent much time in Afghanistan, travelling in a specially converted Hercules transport plane. He and Monktacus 

Here, Trumpet entertains the Pakistani ambassador and his wife in his Bayswater flat.
On the left, Trumpet holds the House of Commons agog with his erudtition and on the right the mighty trunk can just be seen making a point of order at Prime Minister's Question Time.

 
 
Trumpet has become so popular with the masses that he had been incoporated in the Queen's procession to the state opening of parliament. Almost at the risk of upstaging the Queen herself.

Monktacus
A Monkey analogue of the character Spartacus. Freed the monkey slaves in Roman times. Now his main mission is to help Monkey in various crusades. Typically, the slaughter of the hyenas and the war against the monkey god. Monkey is very enamoured of him. The only character who can compete with Trumpet in Monkey's affections. Because of military matters teaking precedence over temporal, Monktacus rarely changes his clothes. Every five years when he needs new armour, the old suit  has to be dissolved off with acid.

Monkey Slave
Slave to the person he calls "madam", actually Girl Monkey. Monkey tends to use him as a slave as well. Monktacus is always trying to encourage him to be free, but he is very happy with his lot. "My life: to serve madam" is his watchword along with "I'll rest when I'm dead". Very occasionally shows signs of melancholia.

Corky the bad clown
This character will add some bite to the storylines. He draws a fine line between genuine nastiness and mischief which will be often blurred. Easily able to hoodwink both Monkey Slave and Girl Monkey to gain entrance to the house. Follows Monkey and Girlfriend all over Europe taking on different ID as fits the country. e.g. Monsieur Le Corque, Herr Kork, Hamish McCorquordale
Simba
A Lion.
Both of these creatures will have to be highly trained. Required to perform stunts with Monkey. Will be seen mostly in flashback. One of the bitter sweet aspects of the series will be flashbacks to happy days in jungle or in circus. But dichotomously, Monkey is happy living the life of a quasi human in an environment where lion and elephant could not function.
Circus Dog
Actually a mongrel part labrador called Grolsch. Whether he ever was in a circus is unclear. Monkey claims to have taught him all sorts of tricks including tightrope walking. In reality only carrying of newspaper and lead will ever be seen. And even these go awry as CD loses lead. Monkey frequently heard saying "I like him" in sentimental tones.
Hyenas
Actually sheep. Monkey appears to be unable to separate simple domestic animals from jungle ones. He doesn't like them.
Lions
Actually cows. Ditto all the above. He doesn't like them.
Aristotle
A Parrot. Appears to have imparted some of the relatively few wise thoughts Monkey has. Monkey frequently heard saying I like him".
Other lions
These will be computer generated as in Gladiator. They attempt to bite Monkey when he is cleaning their cage. Too dangerous to use real ones.
The Monkey God.
This character may be a hallucination or he may take the series into magical realism territory along the lines of MacBeal. Production values will dictate which. If filming can be achieved on the Storr with good weather, he may be treated as magical realism. His size is to remain indeterminate, so he will never be seen in frame with Monkey. Luminescence will surround his head and he will have tusks. There are actually two monkey gods, Banga Chang and Ringa Chinga Nan. Very similar in appearance except only the former has tusks.
Moira Kerr
Singer of couthy Scottish ballads. Monkey sees her as alternately , a beatific saintly figure who sings the song "Cuillin the best song ever written ", or as a lapdog of the monkey dog and thus deserving of deepest condemnation. There may be a hint that these dichotomous positions depend on his sobriety
