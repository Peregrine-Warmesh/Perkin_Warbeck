Detrusor Contractility

The main manifestation of the contractility of the detrusor might be thought to be the detrusor pressure - either as measured as an isometric overactive contraction or a voiding contraction. However in the normal void (especially the female), the urethra distends to the point where the outlet resistance is very low and the power of the detrusor is not seen in the pressure, but rather the flow. So the often seen observation "voids with a low powered detrsuor contraction"  should be scrutinsed carefully.

Fortunately, much work has been done on contractility and again we are indebted to Derek Griffiths. 

In short, the options are:

Stop tests
Simple contractility equations
Complex contractility equations.

Stop tests
In  the stop test, the occlusion of  the flow drives the pressure up the Bladder Output Relation and its maximum value is a measure of contractility. The stop test can either be achieved by the patient deliberately stopping the flow, or by the manual movement of the balloon of a Foley catheter into the orifice. One may ask "why the need for the latter" but  there are arguments that in voluntarily stopping  the stream the patient my de-innervate  the detrusor.
