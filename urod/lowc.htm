The low compliance bladder

The normal detrusor can accept 300 - 600 ml without rise in detrusor presure. In this case there is a marked rise in pressure and the bladder is said to accommodate (pressure decays again) when filling is stopped. 

Compliance is defined as DV/DP. It is has an instantaneous value - the inverse of the tangent to the cystometry curve and also depends on filling rate. One does not make statements of the form "the detrusor compliance was 10ml per cm water" In practice detrusor compliance is usually defined in words (low or high) rather than numbers. 

The meaning of low compliance is a subject of much debate in the urodynamics community. Many spinal injury bladders demonstrate low compliance. The example shown in NOT spinal injury. This patient had detrusor instability at ambulatory studies
NB. The physiologically filled detrusor is always high compliance. The rise in pressure that causes lower compliances is a function of the viscoelastic nature of the detrusor under higher filling rates. i.e. stretch the muscle too fast and it cannot accommodate completely.
