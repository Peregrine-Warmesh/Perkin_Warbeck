Terminology in Urodynamics defined by

The International Continence Society

ICS Terminology Document Scand J Urol Supplementum 114, (1988)

The ICS does not give numerical guidelines for capacity etc

Where these are given below they are local values.

Filling

assess: detrusor activity, sensation, capacity and compliance: Urethral competence

Detrusor Activity

Detrusor may be

Normal/Stable The stable detrusor is one that does not contract during the filling phase while the patient is attempting to inhibit micturition.
Overactive The unstable detrusor is one that is shown to contract spontaneously or on provocation during the filling phase while the patient is attempting to inhibit micturition.
Hyperreflexic Hyperreflexia is defined as overactivity due to disturbance of the nervous control mechanism. Should only be used where there is objective evidence of neurological disorder.
Sensation

Sensation may be

Normal  
Hypersensitive First desire to empty bladder at capacity less than 150 ml
Hyposensitive First desire to empty bladder at capacity greater than 350 ml
Absent  
Capacity
Small < 350 ml
Average 350 < <650
Large > 650 ml
Compliance

Compliance is defined as DV/DP. It is has an instantaneous value - the inverse of the tangent to the cystometry curve and also depends on filling rate. One does not make statements of the form "the detrusor compliance was 10ml per cm water" In practice detrusor compliance is defined as

Low (Hypocompliant)
Normal
High (Hypercompliant)

NB. The physiologically filled detrusor is always high compliance. The rise in pressure that causes lower compliances is a function of the viscoelastic nature of the detrusor under higher filling rates. i.e. stretch the muscle too fast and it cannot accommodate completely.

Urethra

The urethral closure mechanism under filling may be

Normal Maintains a positive closure pressure (Pura - Pves) throughout filling.
Incompetent Allows leakage of urine in the absence of a detrusor contraction. This is termed "genuine stress incontinence"

Voiding studies

assess detrusor contractility and urethral obstruction

During voiding the detrusor may be

acontractile one that cannot be demonstrated to contract during urodynamics
areflexic acontractility due to an abnormality of nervous control
underactive a detrusor contraction of inadequate magnitude and/or duration to effect bladder emptying
normal voluntarily initiated contraction that is sustained and can usually be suppressed voluntarily

During voiding the Urethra may be

  overactive
