Humans Humans are a strange people, private and absurd
They stalk about inflamed by curiosity
Devious they wander
Perpetrators of mysterious notions
They fumble into slippers
Painstakingly they boil up cocoa for reasons of its taste
Their teeth enjoy toast for crunching brings such a nice feeling
They chant "ho ho ho" at funny jokes and like to know awkward things about people in another land
Little animals roam about in chains at their feet
They keep them fluffy and buy them bones because they think them stupid
And are proud that they are better creatures and much cleverer.

Animal Plot Animals greet me as I pass
Squeaks of quaint admiration
But darker creatures that love me not plot insidiously against my very blood
I amble by pretending not to sense their presence as they hop across walls to hide their wicked plans
They contrive to terminate my life quite soon in most unpleasant fashion
But I warn them in tones profound and thunderous
Be it evening frog or dawn light sparrow
I do not suffer torment from feathers or from farrow
The innocent squeak or hushed croak won't crush my living body
For I know fearsome friends most faithful
The fierce dog from the scrapyard's buckled twists
Has shiny fangs that sip blood-reddily
And mangle sinews of slime-clad frog
The next door horse pulverises small birds neath the ruthless hooves of horn
And midnight spiders sing requiem mass to the fallen frogs
And rebellious sparrows who dare to cross my path

The Grand Tobacconer Aha the Grand Tobaconner
'mongst sweet oaks and watermeadows does dwell
who speculates the ways of butterflies
but who wades in the bowels of hell
"kind sir" a gentleman once asked me
observing my ludicrous grin
"what news of the Grand Tobacconner, his own dear kith and kin"
there once was a sewage wastepipe
there once was a village green
there once was a Grand Tobacconer
had he lived he'd have married a queen

It was I
the London wasp at no. 27
only three score years ago
Stinging the old man
lurking in his cap
Buzzing round his pipe
and rejoicing at his shriek

But again i died in agony
because of the boot and the
ground.
My soul explodes in jealously
Inanimacy (I’ve always held)
has no need in reincarnation
(Oh to be a slab)

But here am I
Black and gold -
Dying on a pavement
with the world spinning round me
this wasp in agony
Predestination is an enemy
its limits creativity
And now I am a horse
A nineteenth century horse.

THESE THINGS You are strawberry custard
in a sky blue bowl
You are honey scented Brylcreme
in a champagne glass
You are a white pebble at the
bottom of a pool
on summer solstice dawn
You are lightning in an attic
where starlings never gather
You are a monument to the
Solar system caught in a
lonely jam jar
You are the feast of children
in the Lions head behind
Menagerie bars
You are not you I say
you are these things.

STEAMSHIP TO THE ISLES WITH MY OLD DOG BY MY SIDE AGAIN. STEAMSHIP TO THE ISLES WITH MY OLD DOG BY MY SIDE AGAIN.
Queen Elizabeth the Second invited me to tea
She cooked me ’Palace Surprise’
Because she knew I appreciated fine cuisine:
Scrambled eggs fried in butter, garlic, and
creme de menthe with a garnish of
fresh blue orchids.
We dined in silence and watched
the larks in the clouds before dusk
Afterwards she played her Cello
and sang me a song she composed
About the Church of England.
I gently lit my pipe, and smiling a
sentimental smile asked if she would
Play me the tune about the Church of Scotland
on her harmonica because it always
broughi a romantic tear to my eye.
Before I left to catch the last bus home,
she sang an unaccompanied folk song
in Gaelic about the Free Kirk that
was full of ethnic charm and atmosphere.
Then suddenly the past all flooded
back and | felt as if | was on that
MacBrayne’s steamship to the Isles with
my old dog by my side again -
The day the pistol shot
rang out, the day
she blew her brains
out, the day all life seemed
futile

CLOSING TIME IN BROUGHTY Home from the ’Ship Inn’ on Monday
night, the evening is mellow and
the air is clean
Past the wee cottage I hear behind the
blinds the old man swearing at his wife
to an accompaniment of telly
And old greasy Lizzie’s dirty chipper
with the brown pottery cod slightly
forbidding in the dusty darkened window.
| remember the summer season,
with Lizzie’s old smelly dog tied outside
licking up the melted ice cream from the
hot pavement, and the Glaswegians
queing for pie suppers and pickled onions.
Past the window of 'RF Fashions’ and the
whole display of grey terylene trousers
and nylon shirts | smell the Indian
restaurant with the Broughty
Ferry prices where people go when
they’re pissed.
Climbing up the brae I’m nearly home
Down in Grey Street in the darkness
after the pub the people roar,
while on the hill affluent Presbyterian
Volvo owners lie in bed and snore.

The Tobaconner in a previous life.
